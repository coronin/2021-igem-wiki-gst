# Kana Ren

I participated in various activities of Human Practice. My role in the team was to a linkage between the scientific world in the lab and the real world of daily life.

Through various Human Practice activities, I can say that I gradually began to discover, understand, and contemplate on my community, society, and the world I live in. It is the activities of promoting and popularizing biology that gradually helped me realize that the energy barrier of learning biology varies for different groups of people. So I try to understand the world standing in their shoes. This makes me reflect occasionally and consciously on whether I am inclusive enough in my life, whether infrastructure construction, laws, and regulations have taken these marginalized groups into consideration and respect their human rights, the same as everyone else.

In order to realize the free and all-around development of all people, including our team members and students in Fudan University, as well as people with intellectual disabilities and retired senior citizens. I think these inclusivities are very important.
